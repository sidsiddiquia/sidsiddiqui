About

Sid Siddiqui is a current master's degree candidate at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign studying Business Administration. He has previously completed his Bachelor's degree in the Science of Nursing from Purdue University as well as holds multiple assoicate's degrees in Applied Science, Arts, and Science respectively. His professional experience expands into the fields of healthcare delivery and administration, with special regards to point of care and supervisory roles.

Apart from healthcare, Sid has multiple interests in economics and finance, specifically that of digital currency and the future impact it can hold on financial landscapes.

In his free time, Sid enjoys hiking, camping, fishing, and spending time with his family.

Work Experience

RN Team Leader / Inpatient Services RN
Fresenius Medical Care North America
Dates Employed: September 2015 – Present
Location: Elmhurst, IL 

- Collaborated with over 10 DuPage County, Illinois hospitals to provide inpatient hemodialysis treatments to hundreds of acutely and chronically ill patients with a wide variety of comorbidities.
- Led the administration of prescribed hemodialysis treatment by patient care staff to patients suffering from end stage renal disease in the clinic setting.
- Analyzed and interpreted clinical data in regards to patient’s treatment metrics, anemia, bone and mineral, and protein levels, and adjusted patient’s clinical parameters to optimize treatment metrics and outcomes.
- Collaborated with the care coordination team, working closely with physicians, vascular surgeons, social workers, dietitians, and ancillary medical staff to coordinate and achieve optimal patient outcomes.
- Oversaw hemodialysis treatments in acute care settings such as intensive care units, cardiac telemetry units, medical-surgical units, oncology units, and psychiatric units.
- Managed hemodialysis registered nurses and technicians in initiation, duration, and termination of hemodialysis treatments.
- Monitored patients' response to hemodialysis treatments.
- Reviewed ordered medications by physicians for contraindications with patients' previous medical history.
- Arbitrated problems arising from treatment parameters both medical and psychosocial.
- Performed objective patient assessment pre-treatment and post-treatment.
- Initiated and followed up with proper referrals in regards to access issues to physicians, vascular surgeons, social workers, dietitians, or other ancillary medical staff.

Nursing Supervisor
Lakewood Nursing and Rehab Center/Courtyard Healthcare Center
Dates Employed: February 2012 – October 2015
Location: Berwyn, IL

- Supervised staff registered nurses, licensed practical nurses, and patient aide technicians in the care of patients in both acute and subacute rehabilitation units.
- Collaborated with an interdisciplinary team, which includes physicians, dietary/nutritional services, physical and occupational therapy, social services, case management, and MDS care plan coordinators, to bring about the best outcomes for patients.
- Provided and oversaw direct patient care, medication administrations, treatments, dressing changes, and monitoring of lab values to achieve the best standards of care as set by the offices of Medicare and Medicaid.
- Managed skilled nursing care for patients with complex multi-system disorders ranging from end stage renal disease, including patients on peritoneal dialysis and hemodialysis; cardiovascular disease, including patients with atrial fibrillation, uncontrolled hypertension, and congestive heart failure; cognitive and mental health disorders, including patients with schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, schizoaffective disorder, major depressive disorder; and other medical conditions, such as Parkinson’s disease, Alzheimer’s disease, and dementia.
- Analyzed clinical information in order to determine the best skilled nursing decisions in a variety of situations, while simultaneously and communicating with physicians regarding observed changes in patient status or condition, ongoing patient needs, abnormal lab values, necessary medications reconciliations or adjustments.
- Reconciled medication records against health history in order to identify and correct contraindications.
- Intervened in emergency situations such as rapid response and code blue scenarios as code leader to resuscitate patients in acute cardiac or respiratory failure.
- Educated staff, patients, and families on patient diagnosis, relevant clinical information, lab values, and medications.
